# Sales Playbook — Verdocast B2B Tier

Cold outreach to HR/People/Internal-Comms managers for the £199–£999 office league product. Designed for a 9-day window (today through 10 June, the day before tournament kickoff).

## Target customer profile

The single buyer persona, tightly defined:

- **Title:** Head of People, People Operations Manager, Internal Communications Lead, Culture Manager, Employee Experience Manager, or HR Business Partner. Avoid generalist "HR Director" at large companies (procurement involved).
- **Company size:** 100–500 employees. Below 100: tiny budgets, decision-by-CEO. Above 500: procurement and security review will eat your tournament window.
- **Geography:** UK first. Ireland, Australia, English-speaking EU second. Avoid the US for now — Memorial Day weekend kills US engagement and their World Cup interest is lower.
- **Industry filters (positive):** Tech, media, advertising agencies, design studios, consulting, recruiting, SaaS, fintech (non-banking), education, gaming. These companies expect to spend on culture.
- **Industry filters (negative):** Healthcare (compliance), regulated financial services (lockdown), government, manufacturing (hourly workforce won't engage on phones), legal (slow procurement).
- **Cultural signals (positive):** has run March Madness brackets, mentions Eurovision parties, posts birthday/anniversary celebrations on LinkedIn, runs internal Slack channels for hobbies, has a "fun-stuff" Notion page visible on their careers site.
- **Cultural signals (negative):** "Quiet workplace," last LinkedIn post about employee engagement was 18+ months ago, no visible internal events.

## Where to find them (fastest paths)

1. **LinkedIn Sales Navigator free trial** (30 days) — filter by Function: Human Resources, Seniority: Manager/Director, Company size: 51-500. Save 3 leads per company. **Goal: 100 names by end of Day 1.**
2. **Apollo.io free tier** — 60 free email lookups per month. Use to enrich the LinkedIn names with verified emails.
3. **The "engagement signal" search** — Google `site:linkedin.com "[company name]" March Madness OR bracket OR predictor OR "office tournament"`. Companies that did this in March will say yes to World Cup.
4. **Slack community lurking** — People Operations, Lattice, Culture Amp, and similar Slack groups have job boards and pain channels. Don't spam; observe the language they use to describe their problems.

## The cold-email sequence

Three emails over seven days. Total elapsed effort per prospect: ~3 minutes if your CRM/template is set up.

### Email 1 — initial outreach (Day 0)

**Subject lines to A/B test (4 variants, send 25 of each):**
1. `Office World Cup predictor for {Company}?`
2. `Idea for {Company} before kickoff`
3. `{First name} — quick World Cup idea for {Company}`
4. `5 mins to set up a {Company} office predictor`

**Body template:**

```
Hi {First name},

Saw {specific cultural signal — March Madness bracket, that team-photos post, your culture page line about "we love a good office sweepstake", etc}. Looks like the {Company} team is up for a bit of fun.

I built Verdocast — a turnkey office World Cup predictor. Branded join link, employees predict each match, live leaderboard, admin dashboard. About 5 minutes to set up.

For a team your size, it's £499 for up to 250 employees (so ~£2 each) for the whole tournament.

Worth a 90-second Loom? Happy to also do a quick 5-min screen-share — Thursday 2:00 or Friday 11:00 work?

— {Your name}
{verdocast.com}
```

**Rules:**
- The first line MUST reference something specific to their company. Generic = deleted.
- No more than 90 words total.
- One CTA, with a specific time offer. Don't say "let me know."
- Don't attach anything. No PDFs.
- Plain text only — fancy HTML mail gets flagged as marketing.

### Email 2 — follow-up (Day 3)

**Same thread, no new subject.** Reply to your own email.

```
Hi {First name} — quick bump in case it got buried.

11 June kicks off, so timing matters either way. Two-line summary: branded predictor for {Company}, set up in 5 mins, £499 for ≤250 staff.

90-second demo: {Loom link}

If not a fit, no problem — just say the word and I'll stop.

— {Your name}
```

### Email 3 — breakup (Day 7)

**Same thread.**

```
Hi {First name} — last note from me on this so you're not stuck with reminders.

Going quiet now. If office World Cup predictor becomes useful next week, just reply to this thread and I'll get you live same day.

All the best for the tournament,
{Your name}
```

**Why breakup emails work:** they trigger reciprocity. Reply rates on breakup emails are 2–3× the second follow-up. People feel guilty saying nothing when you've offered to go away.

## Reply playbook

### Reply: "Sounds interesting, send more info"

**Don't send more info.** Reply:

> Easiest is a 5-min screen-share — let me show you live and answer anything. {Calendly link} or just pick a time: Wed 3:00 or Thu 10:30?

Document-attached replies almost never convert. Live demos do.

### Reply: "How does pricing work?"

> One-time payment for the whole tournament. £199 ≤50 employees, £499 ≤250, £999 ≤1,000. No subscription. Want me to send a 5-min Loom or just do a quick screen-share?

Always push to demo.

### Reply: "We're not interested"

> No problem — appreciate you replying. If you know another People person in your network who might want it for their team, would massively appreciate the intro.

A polite ask-for-referral converts ~5% of declines into warm intros.

## The 5-minute demo (Zoom or Loom)

Structure, with timings:

| Time | Beat |
|---|---|
| 0:00–0:20 | "I built this in two weeks because I wanted a predictor for my own group — here's what it does." Land the relatability hook first. |
| 0:20–1:30 | **Admin flow:** show the buy → org-setup → league-creation → invite link in real time. Make the speed visible. |
| 1:30–3:00 | **Employee flow:** click the join link → fill name → predictions grid → submit a few. Show the "saved" indicator. |
| 3:00–3:50 | **Leaderboard:** show how points appear, click into a member, show predictions-vs-actual. |
| 3:50–4:30 | **Admin dashboard:** member count, prediction completion, top scorers. |
| 4:30–5:00 | "Want me to get you set up today? £499 for your team size. I'll send a Stripe link in this chat right now." |

**Critical:** at 5:00, do not keep talking. Sit in the silence. The buyer fills it with either yes, no, or an objection. All three are progress.

## Objection handlers

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Too expensive" | "For your team that's about £2 per employee for a month of engagement — about the price of a coffee per person. What's your typical budget for these things?" |
| "We don't have time to set up" | "Five minutes. The demo you just watched showed the full setup live. I can sit on a call with you while you set it up." |
| "Our team isn't into football" | "Some won't engage — that's fine. Even with 30% participation at your size that's 75 people. The ones who do care will love it; the rest just ignore the join email." |
| "IT needs to approve" | "It's a public website, no installation, no SSO required, no data integration. Same approval surface as Eventbrite or Calendly." |
| "We did March Madness through Slack" | "Perfect — you already know the engagement pattern. Verdocast is the World Cup version, slightly more sophisticated leaderboard. £2 per employee." |
| "Can we trial it first?" | "Yes — buy now, full refund if you cancel before kickoff on 11 June. You'll have a working league by the end of this call to share with the team and check it works for them." |
| "Send a proposal" | "Sure — what specifically do you need in writing? Most buyers I work with skip that step. £499 covers up to 250 staff, one-off payment, refundable until kickoff." |
| "We'll get back to you" | "Totally understand. The reason I push for a today-decision is that we have 9 days to kickoff and live setup takes 5 minutes. If you want, I'll send a Stripe link now — you don't have to action it today, but it's there if you want to move quickly. Sound fair?" |

## Closing

When they say yes:
1. **Send a Stripe Payment Link in the call's chat.** Stripe → Payments → Payment Links → create one-time link for the right tier. Have all three pre-created in your Stripe Dashboard before any demo.
2. Stay on the call until they've paid. "Take 30 seconds — I'll wait."
3. After payment lands, share-screen the onboarding flow with them so the league is live before you hang up.
4. End with: "I'll check in with you on Wednesday to see how the joining is going. Anything I should know about your team's quirks?"

**Realistic conversion math at 100 emails:** 8-12 replies, 4-7 demos, 2-4 closes. At Pro tier (£499 avg) that's £1,000–£2,000 from your first 100 emails. Send 200 in week 1 and you've cleared £3K. Not life-changing, but a working business by week one.
